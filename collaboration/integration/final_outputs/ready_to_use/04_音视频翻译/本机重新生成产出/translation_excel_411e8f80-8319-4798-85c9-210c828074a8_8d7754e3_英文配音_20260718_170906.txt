The Little Firefly's Starlight Gift
There lives a little firefly named Diandian in the forest.
The light at the tail of other fireflies is clear and bright, only Diandian's light is weak and dim, like a spark that is about to go out.
When night falls, my friends fly through the woods carrying bright little lanterns, lighting up the paths and guiding small animals heading home for the night. Diandian can only follow silently behind them, filled with disappointment in his heart.
It always thinks that it is the most useless firefly in the forest.
On a late autumn night, the autumn wind is slightly cool, and the night is pitch black.
The little squirrel deep in the forest got lost accidentally. The dark woods made it shrink back into a tree hole out of fear, and it began to cry quietly.
The friends all flew far away to illuminate the main road in the forest, and no one noticed the helpless little squirrel in the corner.
Diandian heard the crying and flew towards the tree hole without hesitation.
It tries its best to emit its faint light, the tiny spot of light sways gently, and softly illuminates the grass around the tree hole.
There is no dazzling brightness, yet it is enough to dispel the darkness before my eyes.
Diandian flutters slowly around the tree hollow, gently comforts the flustered little squirrel, and gradually guides it to the path home.
Though the faint light is dim, it steadily illuminates the narrow dirt road underfoot.
The little squirrel gradually stopped being afraid. Following the faint glimmer of light, it walked step by step out of the deep woods and returned home smoothly.
When parting, the little squirrel thanked him sincerely, saying this was the warmest light it had ever seen.
At this moment, Diandian suddenly understands that brightness never comes in different sizes—even a wisp of faint light can warm people's hearts and illuminate the road ahead.
The evening breeze blows through the woods, the tiny lights on their tails flicker gently, still not dazzling, yet extraordinarily gentle.
From then on, it no longer felt inferior, always holding up its own little lantern, guarding every small animal in the forest that needed help, and piecing together the warmest night of the forest with its faint light.
